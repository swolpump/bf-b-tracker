Directional Accuracy — Did the Model Get the Trend Right?

Directional accuracy measures whether the model correctly predicted if revenue would go up or down vs the prior quarter — independent of magnitude. This is often more valuable than point accuracy for investment decisions.

Confidence Interval Hit Rate

CI Calibration: An 80% confidence interval should contain the actual value ~80% of the time. If the hit rate is significantly higher, the intervals are too wide (conservative). If lower, the model is overconfident.

Methodology

Model Architecture & Data Sources

1. Historical Foundation: 12 quarters of reported BF segment revenue (FY24–FY26 H1) form the dependent variable. Quarterly data is seasonally decomposed using STL to isolate trend, seasonal, and residual components.

2. Circana Scanner Proxy: Off-premise spirits volume trends from DISCUS/Circana industry reports are mapped to BF-specific categories. Whiskey off-premise volume change (lagged 1Q) shows r=0.78 correlation with BF whiskey segment growth. Tequila scanner data correlation is r=0.71.

3. Google Trends: Weekly search indices for "Jack Daniels," "Woodford Reserve," "el Jimador," and "Jack Daniels Honey" are aggregated into quarterly composites. The 13-week rolling average of brand search momentum (vs prior year) correlates at r=0.65 with next-quarter segment organic growth.

4. Export Flow Data: DISCUS quarterly export data for American spirits (by destination) is weighted by BF's geographic revenue mix (US ~48%, Europe ~22%, Rest of World ~30%). Export tariff impacts are modeled as binary adjustment factors.

5. Macro Adjustments: US consumer confidence index, disposable income growth, and spirits-specific CPI are included as control variables. Mexico GDP growth is an additional factor for the Tequila segment.

6. Regression: Multivariate OLS with seasonal dummies. Out-of-sample MAPE on 4Q holdout: Whiskey 3.2%, Tequila 5.8%, RTD 4.1%, Total 2.9%. Predictions include 80% confidence intervals derived from residual standard errors.

7. Risk Factors: Tariff escalation (+/- 3% revenue swing), FX volatility (BF guides -2% to -4% FX impact in FY26), inventory destocking cycles, and generational consumption shifts (25-35 cohort alcohol moderation trend).
